FDIR LeWM Training Board

A local instrument for the fault-detection experiment: nominal telemetry training, state-level fault rollouts, latent surprise detection, calibrated multi-fault benchmarks, and comparison against a simple observation z-score energy baseline. Training curves and the live run are logged to WandB when the trainer runs with W&B enabled.

Detection Signal

This is the illustrative single-rollout trace. LeWM surprise is squared latent prediction error; the baseline is raw observation z-score energy under the nominal dataset normalizer. Dashed lines show the local pre-fault relative threshold for this trace.

Fault-Mode Benchmark

Nominal-calibrated thresholds are evaluated across multiple seeds and fault modes. Detection means the score crosses threshold within the configured post-fault window; pre-fault alarms are counted separately.

Training Process

A compact map of the FDIR path from simulator data to the anomaly score.

Nominal dynamicsEight telemetry states evolve around setpoints with stable coupled linear dynamics.
Nominal datasetEpisodes store noisy observations, true hidden state, and constant nominal one-hot actions.
EncoderAn MLP maps each 8D telemetry vector into a 192D LeWM embedding.
AR predictorA conditional transformer predicts the next latent from history and action embeddings.
Fault rolloutFaults modify state evolution, then propagate through coupled channels.
SurpriseHigh prediction error flags dynamics that are unlikely under the nominal-trained model.
